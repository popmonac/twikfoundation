Google's Commitment to Africa

Earlier this year, Google announced a major commitment to train 3 million students, teachers, and youth across Africa in digital and artificial intelligence (AI) skills over the next five years.

Through this initiative, Tech with Khalid Foundation (TWiK) and Electrons are helping Nigerian universities educate their students about this life-changing opportunity.

Students will get free access to Google's most advanced AI model ‚Äî Gemini Pro ‚Äî for 12 months.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the program

Is the program really free? ‚ñº
Yes! Access to Gemini Pro, all learning materials, mentorship sessions, and certification are completely free for selected students.
How much time do I need to commit? ‚ñº
We recommend 5-10 hours per week for optimal learning. The program is flexible to work around your class schedule.
Who is eligible for this program? ‚ñº
All Nigerian university students (100-600 level) from any department can apply. No prior coding or AI experience is required.
How will I be tracked and recognized? ‚ñº
Your university's Institutional Coordinator will monitor your progress. Tech with Khalid Foundation and Electrons will send comprehensive reports to Google highlighting top performers.
Do I need coding experience? ‚ñº
You'll receive a Google certificate, your work may be recommended to Google for recognition, and you'll be considered for career opportunities, internships, scholarships, and grants through Google and partner organizations.
